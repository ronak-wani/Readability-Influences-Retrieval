Readability Influences Retrieval

An Investigation on Influence of Readability Levels on RAG Systems

Authors: Ronak Wani, Matthew Kiszla, Shreelaxmi Malawade, Glenn D'souza

Objective and Goal of the project

To investigate the influence of readability level of document corpus on Retrieval augmented generation (RAG).

Explore the average ranking of retrieved documents based on linguistic complexity

Documents share the same context but differ in sentence structure, and vocabulary

Explore how the generation from these documents are affected

System Overview

  • Step 1: We have documents with different levels of linguistic complexity, i.e., elemantary, intermediate and advance. These documents are embedded using embedding models, which makes them as vector representations that a model can search through.
  • Step 2: Then, we pass a query, the system retrieves and ranks some of these documents as most relevant. It ranks the documents of a particular complexity level like advance text is ranked higher than others, even if all are relevant.
  • Step 3: The top‑ranked documents are passed into a generation model that uses them to produce an output, like a summary and to check whether the ranking bias has any impact on the output.

Conclusion

  • For dense embeddings, we saw that the Intermediate Level document had a weighted rank score higher than the Elementary Level Document and the Advanced Level Document.
  • We also saw that that the sparse vector models preferred the Elementary Level Documents.
  • The readability of the document also influences the readability of the generated responses.

    • Overall, It can be determined that the Readability of the Document Corpus can influence the RAG pipeline in both retrieval and generation​
